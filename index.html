Nelson Mandela

The man who had the big heart for Forgiviness

Nelson Mandela a revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999

Here's a tribute of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata, Cape Province
  • 1933 - Mandela began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo
  • 1939 - Mandela began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare
  • 1940 - Mandela found that Jongintaba had arranged marriages for him and Justice; dismayed, they fled to Johannesburg via Queenstown, arriving in April 1941.
  • 1941 - Secured a job as an articled clerk at the law firm of Witkin
  • 1943 - After passing his BA exams he returned to Johannesburg. Mandela began studying law at the University of the Witwatersrand, where he was the only black African student and faced racism.
  • 1944 - Mandela met Evelyn Mase, a trainee nurse and ANC activist from Engcobo, Transkei they entered into a relationship and got married.
  • 1947 - Mandela was elected to the executive committee of the ANC's Transvaal Province branch
  • 1952 - Mandela was arrested under the Suppression of Communism Act and stood trial as one of the 21 accused.
  • 1956 - Mandela was again arrested alongside most of the ANC national executive, and accused of "high treason" against the state.
  • 1964 - Justice De Wet found Mandela and two of his co-accused guilty of political offenses and he was jailed and later transfered to Robin Island,remaining there for the next 18 years.
  • 1975 - Mandela had become a Class A prisoner, which allowed him greater numbers of visits and letters
  • 1982 - Mandela was transferred to Pollsmoor Prison in Tokai, Cape Town
  • 1988 - Mandela's 70th birthday attracted international attention, including a tribute concert at London's Wembley Stadium that was televised and watched by an estimated 200 million viewers.
  • 1988 - Mandela was moved to Victor Verster Prison near Paarl
  • 1990 - Mandela was released unconditionally thereafter, for the first time in 20 years, photographs of Mandela were allowed to be published in South Africa.
  • 1994 - He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.
  • 1999 - Mandela retired after 5 years and lead a quiet family life
  • 2013 - Mandela died at the age of 95, at his home in Houghton, surrounded by his family.

    • “Nelson Mandela will forever be remembered as more than a political leader, he was a moral leader, He spent much of his life standing against the injustice of apartheid. When that fight was won, he inspired us again by his capacity to forgive and reconcile his country. While the world may never see another Nelson Mandela, he has inspired countless men and women throughout the world to live more courageous and honest lives.”
